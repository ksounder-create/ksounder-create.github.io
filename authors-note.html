# Author’s Note: The Recovery of a Lost Central Principle What is presented on this site is not the invention of a new belief about life. It is the recovery of a principle that was always there, now supported by an extraordinary body of experimental evidence. Modern molecular biology and the Indic Knowledge System arrived at the same conclusion by very different paths: that Nature operates through a single, pervasive law, and that complexity is its expression rather than its contradiction. In biology, this principle was buried under decades of fragmented data and inherited assumptions. In philosophy, it was obscured by layers of symbolism and ritual. Each preserved a shadow of the same truth, and each was incomplete on its own. Together, they made it possible to recover the law that governs life. I did not approach this problem optimistically. I approached it with the assumption that if laws existed, they should already have been visible — and that their absence meant something was deeply wrong in how the field was framed. That skepticism made it possible to question assumptions that most of biology had stopped examining. I began by searching the scientific literature for one fully consistent molecular fact. None existed. The literature was saturated with paradoxes, contradictions, and context-dependent explanations — the opposite of what a lawful system should produce. Only when I abandoned the Central Dogma of molecular biology and treated paradoxes as signals of missing laws did the rules begin to appear. What emerged was a single governing principle whose repeated application generated the full complexity of living systems. Nature has one law; everything else is its expression. The novelty here is not the principle itself — it is that it is now supported by an extraordinary body of experimental data, across thousands of independent studies. The ancient insight and the modern evidence have finally met. This is how lost knowledge returns.
